Introduction

Hydrogen fuel cells are one of the new technologies being researched as an alternative to the widely available internal combustion engines in many fields of transportation. This is
in hopes of finding a greener solution which has a similar range to internal combustion engines which cannot be said about battery vehicles as they known for having ‘range anxiety’.

How does a hydrogen fuel cell work

The widely used hydrogen fuel cell for transportation systems is the PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) fuel cell. A hydrogen fuel cell is like a mix of both internal combustion
engines and battery powered electric vehicles. 
       

It firstly takes the hydrogen from the hydrogen tank.

It then takes air from the vents into the fuel stack. 

There is a reaction using electrolysis between hydrogen and air to produce electricity and water vapour.

The electricity produced is used to turn an electric motor which is how the vehicle is powered.

Water vapour is emitted out of the car which is the waste product.(1)

The process starts with the hydrogen’s electron is taken by the anode causing the hydrogen atom to become an ion. The electrons travel through a wire to create a current to 
power the motor. The hydrogen ions within the electrolyte travels from the anode to cathode where it regains its electrons and 2 hydrogen atoms and 1 oxygen atom react to 
form water vapour.

Ways to make the fuel cell more efficient

One way of making a hydrogen fuel cell more efficient is temperature control. The optimal temperature range is between 60 and 80 degrees Celsius and if it is not with this 
range then it can lead to the decrease of performance output, decrease in the efficiency or even damage to the fuel stack. To increase the efficiency, the use of advanced 
temperature control techniques such as co-flow and counter flow systems.

Another way of making a hydrogen fuel cell more efficient is humidity control. This is important as the protons in the electrolysis process must be hydrated to the correct 
amount. To increase the efficiency, the use of humidifiers or recirculating the water.

Another way of making a hydrogen fuel cell more efficient is to control the pressure. This is important especially for where the electrical loads. If the pressure is too low, 
then the supply of the reactant is too little which means that the power output will be reduced and if there is too much pressure then it will break the membrane plate which 
will lead to the failure of the fuel stack. To increase the efficiency, the use of advanced pressure management systems such as utilising multiple stages or optimising the 
pressure gradients to reduce energy consumption.

The final way of making a hydrogen fuel cell more efficient is by optimising the flow rates. This is important as it allows the maximum utilisation of the catalyst and reduces 
the chances of too much or too little reactant. To increase the efficiency, the use of computational fluid dynamics will minimise the chances of waste or not enough reactant.
